To manage your progress without RPE, you need to treat your workout log like a financial budget. **Total Volume (TV)** is your "spending." If you overspend (spike the volume), you go into "fatigue debt." If you spend steadily (2% to 5%), you build "muscle equity."

Here is the breakdown of why these numbers matter and how to calculate them.

---

### 1. The Logic: Why 2-5% vs. 10%?

#### **The 2% to 5% Range (The "Sweet Spot")**

This is the ideal weekly increase for **Progressive Overload**.

* **The Cause:** This small increase provides enough "stress" to signal your body to grow, but not so much that it damages your recovery capacity.
* **The Effect:** You can sustain this for 4–8 weeks before needing a break (deload). This leads to long-term, injury-free strength gains.

#### **The >10% Jump (The "Red Zone")**

A 10% jump in a single session is a massive shock to your Central Nervous System (CNS) and connective tissues.

* **The Risk:** Muscle recovers faster than tendons. A 10% jump might feel "okay" in your muscles, but your joints and tendons take a beating.
* **The Result:** This is where **Performance Degradation** starts. You might hit the numbers today, but next week you'll likely feel sluggish, "heavy," or experience joint "niggles."

---

### 2. The Formula for Total Volume (TV)

To find your volume, you multiply everything together for a specific exercise or a whole muscle group.

$$Total Volume (TV) = Sets \times Reps \times Weight$$

#### **How to Calculate the % Change:**

To see if your jump was too big, use this formula:


$$\% \text{ Change} = \left( \frac{\text{This Week's TV} - \text{Last Week's TV}}{\text{Last Week's TV}} \right) \times 100$$

**Example:**

* **Last Week:** 3 sets $\times$ 10 reps $\times$ 50kg = **1,500kg**
* **This Week:** 3 sets $\times$ 10 reps $\times$ 55kg = **1,650kg**
* **Calculation:** $((1650 - 1500) / 1500) \times 100 = \mathbf{10\%}$

> **Note:** Even though you only added 5kg, your **Total Volume** jumped by 10%. This is on the edge of the "Red Zone."

---

### 3. What to do based on the numbers?

| If the % Change is... | Meaning | **Action Plan** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **0% to 2%** | Maintenance / Slow Progress | **Stay the course.** This is great for "bad sleep" weeks or when life is stressful. |
| **2% to 5%** | **Optimal Growth** | **Maintain.** This is your target. Keep adding a tiny bit of weight or 1-2 extra reps. |
| **5% to 10%** | Aggressive Push | **Monitor.** Do not increase again next week. Let your body "catch up" to this new load. |
| **>10% Jump** | Dangerous Spike | **Reduce.** Drop the weight or sets by 5% next session to avoid "crashing" in 2 weeks. |

---

### 4. How to detect "Performance Degradation" using this formula

If your **Total Volume** drops for two sessions in a row (e.g., you can't hit the reps you did last week), your body is telling you it is **Under-Recovered**.

* **The Fix:** Take a **Deload Week**. Reduce your Total Volume by **30% to 50%** for one week. This allows your "fatigue debt" to clear so you can start the 2-5% climb again.
